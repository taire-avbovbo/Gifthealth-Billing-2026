Oversupply vs invoiced
Purpose: GiftHealth asked Hugo to clarify how invoice hours relate to Assembled (workforce monitoring) hours. This report walks through the billing equation, reconciles every line item, and provides a calendar view of training vs. production for February 2026.
Hugo provided 48,853 total hours but only invoiced 43,660 — a gap of 5,193 hours (11.9%)
Invoiced hours only include production + training for the contracted headcount. Hugo staffs additional "buffer" agents (368 total vs. 328 billable) and absorbs their hours. Assembled also includes buffer agents in production, which is why Assembled scheduled hours (35,573) can exceed contracted production hours (22,480). Assembled only tracks agents once they enter production/nesting — training hours never appear in Assembled.

Key terms & billing definitions
Maximum contracted hours
Max hours Hugo can bill. Formula: billable agents × working days × hrs/day.
Training hours
Hours contracted agents spend in ~2-week training. Not tracked in Assembled.
Production hours (contracted)
Hours contracted agents spend in production/nesting after training. Tracked in Assembled.
Total invoiced hours
Training (contracted) + Production (contracted). What GiftHealth pays for.
Assembled hours
Hours in Assembled (workforce monitoring). Only captures production/nesting. Includes contracted AND buffer agents. Includes overtime.
Buffer agents
Extra agents above contracted levels for SLA coverage. Never invoiced. Their production appears in Assembled.
Overtime hours
Extra hours requested by GH (Kwik Pen launch). 20% premium. Tracked in Assembled.
Total provided hours
Everything Hugo delivers: Assembled + contracted training + buffer training. Always exceeds invoiced.
